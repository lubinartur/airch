# Continuity

## Definition

**Continuity** is time as a place you can stand.

It is not a calendar. It is not a feed. It is not a timeline of events.

It is the through-line of a person's life — the arc that makes the present moment understandable.

---

## Core Question

> How did I get here?

---

## What Continuity Answers

- How did I get here?
- What has actually changed over months and years?
- What keeps repeating?
- What is gaining momentum?
- What has quietly faded?
- What is the current direction, based on everything AIRCH knows?
- What decisions from a year ago are still shaping today?

---

## Why It Matters

Everything else in AIRCH lives mostly in the present.

Today is now. Dialogue is now. Spaces remember within domains.

Continuity is the missing axis. It makes AIRCH feel like it understands the user's life over years — not just over days.

Without Continuity, AIRCH can claim to know a person, but never prove it.

With Continuity, the user can see that AIRCH has been paying attention across time.

---

## What Continuity Is Not

- a calendar
- a feed of past events
- a log of every transaction, workout, or conversation
- a highlight reel
- a journal
- a timeline widget

Continuity is not a record. It is an understanding.

---

## Structure

**The arc**
A high-level read on the user's trajectory over a meaningful period. Written by AIRCH. Not generated from a template — genuinely constructed from patterns, decisions, and Memory.

Example: "The last six months have been a consolidation. Three projects became one. Spending stabilized. You stopped asking about escaping and started asking about building."

**Key shifts**
The moments where something meaningfully changed — a decision made, a pattern broken, a new direction taken. AIRCH identifies these from Memory, not from the user's self-reporting.

**Repeating patterns**
What keeps recurring across time. Not as criticism — as information. "This is the third time this has happened. The previous two ended the same way."

**What is emerging**
Signals that something is changing now. New patterns forming. Old ones weakening.

**Current read**
AIRCH's single assessment of where the user appears to be in their arc right now.

---

## Tone in Continuity

Continuity speaks more slowly than Today or Dialogue.

It is reflective. It looks backward to explain the present.

It does not moralize. It does not say "you should have." It says "here is what the data shows."

It uses honest uncertainty where appropriate: "It looks like..." "The pattern suggests..." "This might be..."

---

## Continuity and the Other Concepts

- **Today** uses Continuity to understand whether today's signal fits a long-term pattern.
- **Mirror** is drawn partly from Continuity — stable facts about the user emerge from their life arc.
- **Direction** is partly defined by looking at Continuity — where has this person been heading?
- **Threads** often become visible through Continuity — a story that spans months only becomes a story in retrospect.

---

## Access Pattern

Continuity is not opened daily. It is accessed when the user wants to understand a longer arc — before a major decision, after a significant period, or when they feel disoriented about where they are.

AIRCH may also surface Continuity insights in Today or Dialogue when a pattern that has been building finally becomes clear enough to name.
