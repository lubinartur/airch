# Spaces

## Definition

**Spaces** are not projects.

They are rooms AIRCH has been keeping for different domains of life.

---

## Core Idea

Entering a Space should feel like walking into a room that has been prepared for you — where AIRCH has already gathered the relevant memory, open questions, tensions, and next moves.

Not like opening a folder. Not like loading a board. Like returning to a place that knows you.

---

## Core Question

> What is happening in this part of my life?

---

## What a Space Contains

- **Domain memory** — what AIRCH knows about this area of life
- **Open questions** — unresolved things that deserve attention
- **Momentum signal** — is this domain moving, stalled, or steady?
- **Recent events** — what has happened here lately
- **Connected Threads** — cross-domain stories that touch this Space
- **AIRCH's current read** — one line on what this Space is telling AIRCH

A Space does not show everything. It shows what matters in this domain right now.

---

## Examples of Spaces

- Finance
- Health
- Work / Startup
- Learning
- Personal
- A specific project that has grown large enough to deserve its own room
- Ducati (or any other meaningful life domain)

Spaces are not predefined by the system. They emerge from how the user lives — and AIRCH may propose a new Space when a domain reaches sufficient weight in memory.

---

## Principles

**A Space is a living room, not a folder.**
It should show what has been changing, not just what is stored.

**It connects facts into meaning.**
Not: here are your transactions. But: your spending in this area has shifted.

**It helps the user re-enter quickly.**
Someone returning to a Space after two weeks should understand the current state in under 30 seconds.

**It shows momentum, not just status.**
Not a static list. A sense of direction — what is growing, what is stalling, what needs a decision.

**AIRCH speaks within the Space.**
One line at the top: AIRCH's current read on this domain. Not a report. A sentence.

---

## Space Entry Point

From Today or navigation: the Space Card.

Shows:
- Name
- AIRCH's one-line read
- Momentum indicator (quietly, not as a progress bar)
- Last meaningful activity

Nothing more. Depth is inside.

---

## Spaces Are Not

- databases
- boards
- lists
- dashboards
- folders
- Notion pages
- Jira projects
- CRM pipelines

---

## How Spaces Evolve

A Space is not created and then static.

Over time:
- Memory accumulates within it
- AIRCH notices patterns specific to this domain
- Open questions build up and resolve
- Threads connect it to other Spaces
- The Space itself becomes part of the user's Continuity

AIRCH may suggest archiving a Space that has gone dormant, or creating a new one when a domain becomes significant enough.
