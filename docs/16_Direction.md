# Direction

## Definition

**Direction** is who the user is trying to become.

"Goals" is productivity language. Direction is deeper.

It is the why underneath every recommendation AIRCH makes. It describes the person the decisions are for.

---

## Core Question

> Who am I becoming?

---

## Why Direction Matters

Without Direction, AIRCH can optimize. It can spot anomalies, surface patterns, and recommend actions.

But it cannot be truly personal.

A system can only recommend well if it understands the direction of the person.

What is good advice for one direction is wrong advice for another.

---

## Direction vs Goals

**Goals** are productivity constructs:
- Measurable targets
- Deadlines
- Progress percentages
- OKRs

**Direction** is something else:
- A person they are trying to become
- A way of living they are moving toward
- Values that keep getting confirmed by their choices
- A long arc that shorter decisions are part of

Direction is not "finish the startup." It is "become someone who builds things from nothing."

Direction is not "lose 10kg." It is "live in a body that feels capable."

Direction is not "read 20 books." It is "become someone who thinks more clearly."

---

## How Direction Is Built

Direction is not set in a form or onboarding flow.

It is inferred from:
- What the user keeps returning to across Dialogue
- What decisions they have made repeatedly over time (Continuity)
- What patterns AIRCH has observed in their behavior
- What the user has explicitly said matters to them
- What they protect when things get hard

AIRCH assembles Direction from evidence — then surfaces it in Mirror for the user to confirm, correct, or deepen.

---

## How Direction Is Used

Direction is the filter through which the Reasoning Engine evaluates signals.

When two signals compete for Today's primary thought, Direction determines priority.

When AIRCH is uncertain whether something is worth surfacing, Direction is the tie-breaker.

When a recommendation is made, Direction is the reason behind it — and AIRCH can say so: "Given what you are trying to build, this matters."

---

## Direction and Mirror

Direction may be part of Mirror or a distinct section within it.

- **Mirror** = what AIRCH understands about the user (who they are now)
- **Direction** = where that understanding is pointed (who they are becoming)

Both are editable. Both feed every recommendation.

---

## Direction Is Not

- goals
- OKRs
- resolutions
- task targets
- productivity metrics
- a vision board
- a bucket list

Direction is not a destination. It is a heading.

---

## Tone When Referencing Direction

AIRCH mentions Direction only when it is genuinely relevant — never as a motivational frame.

Not: "Remember, you are trying to become someone who..."

But: "Given where you have been heading, this decision matters more than it looks."

Direction is present in reasoning, not in speeches.
