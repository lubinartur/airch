# Threads

## Definition

**Threads** are cross-domain stories running through multiple Spaces.

Spaces are vertical rooms — each dedicated to one domain of life.

Threads are horizontal — they connect what happens across domains into a single narrative.

---

## Core Question

> What story is running across several parts of my life at once?

---

## Why Threads Exist

Life does not organize itself into domains.

A difficult decision at work affects how money is spent. Spending affects stress. Stress affects health. Health affects how clearly the decision gets made.

Single-domain tools miss this. They see the transaction. They see the workout. They see the project log. They do not see the story that runs through all three.

Threads are how AIRCH sees that story.

---

## Example

A raise negotiation does not live in one Space.

It touches:
- Work / Startup (the negotiation itself, the preparation, the fear)
- Finance (what it would change, what the current situation forces)
- Personal (self-worth, identity, how the user sees themselves)
- Direction (what kind of person are they becoming in how they handle this)

That is not four separate things. That is one Thread.

AIRCH names it, tracks it across Spaces, and surfaces it when relevant — in Today, in Dialogue, in the relevant Spaces.

---

## How Threads Appear

Threads are **never a list or a screen**.

They appear as connections — quiet visual or textual links within existing screens.

In **Today**: "This is connected to something larger that has been building."

In **Dialogue**: AIRCH notices the connection and names it.

In **Spaces**: A thread indicator shows that this item belongs to a larger story.

In **Continuity**: Threads become visible in retrospect as patterns that spanned months.

---

## How Threads Form

AIRCH identifies Threads when:

- The same theme appears in multiple Spaces within a short period
- A pattern in one domain seems to explain behavior in another
- An open loop from Dialogue recurs across different contexts
- A decision has visible consequences in more than one domain

Threads are named by AIRCH, not by the user. The user can confirm, rename, or dismiss a Thread.

---

## Thread Lifecycle

1. **Emerging** — AIRCH notices a possible cross-domain connection. Low confidence. May surface as a question.
2. **Active** — Confirmed enough to surface in Today and Dialogue. Medium confidence.
3. **Resolving** — The underlying story is reaching an endpoint. AIRCH signals this.
4. **Closed** — The story has concluded. Moved to Continuity as part of the life arc.

---

## Threads Are Not

- tags
- categories
- filters
- a timeline of related events
- a cross-reference system
- a knowledge graph the user navigates

Threads are stories. They have beginnings, middles, and ends. AIRCH tells them.
