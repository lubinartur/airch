# AIRCH Information Architecture

## Structure

```text
AIRCH OS
│
├── Today                    — What deserves attention now?
│
├── Dialogue                 — Continuing conversations with AIRCH
│
├── Spaces                   — Rooms for domains of life
│   ├── Finance
│   ├── Health
│   ├── Work / Startup
│   ├── Learning
│   └── Personal
│
├── Continuity               — Life arc / how you got here
│
├── Mirror                   — What AIRCH believes about you
│
├── Direction                — Who you are becoming
│
├── Threads                  — Cross-domain life stories
│
└── Stillness                — [No screen. System principle.]
```

---

## Concept Levels

### Primary screens (daily use)
- **Today** — opened every morning
- **Dialogue** — ongoing access throughout the day
- **Spaces** — entered when focusing on a domain

### Secondary screens (weekly or on-demand)
- **Continuity** — when the user wants to understand a longer arc
- **Mirror** — when the user wants to inspect or correct what AIRCH knows
- **Direction** — when the user wants to reconsider priorities

### Connective layer (no dedicated screen)
- **Threads** — surfaced within Today, Dialogue, and Spaces; never a separate list
- **Stillness** — a principle applied across all screens, never a feature
- **Presence** — AIRCH's character, expressed through every word it says

---

## Concept Relationships

**Today** depends on everything:
- Spaces (domain status)
- Dialogue (open questions)
- Continuity (long patterns)
- Mirror (what AIRCH knows)
- Direction (what matters long-term)
- Reasoning Engine (what to surface today)

**Dialogue** depends on:
- Memory (context between sessions)
- Mirror (who the user is)
- Previous conversations (continuity of thought)
- Open loops (unresolved threads)

**Spaces** depend on:
- Domain memory (events, decisions, facts)
- Threads (cross-space connections)
- Continuity (how this domain has evolved)
- Open questions (what is unresolved here)

**Continuity** depends on:
- Long-term memory
- Milestones and events
- Repeated patterns
- Behavioral shifts over time

**Mirror** depends on:
- Observed patterns
- Explicit user corrections
- Stable preferences and values
- Identity signals from Dialogue and Spaces

**Direction** depends on:
- Values
- Long-term commitments
- Repeated choices
- What the user says matters

---

## Navigation Principle

The user should never feel lost between concepts.

AIRCH moves between them naturally:
- Today surfaces a thread → tapping it opens the relevant Space or Dialogue.
- Dialogue surfaces a pattern → Mirror shows where it lives.
- A Space shows a decision → Continuity shows its history.

Navigation should feel like moving through a familiar building, not switching between apps.

---

## What Never Becomes a Screen

- **Stillness** — It is a principle, not a destination.
- **Presence** — It is AIRCH's voice, not a settings panel.
- **Threads** — They appear as connections within other screens, never as a list.
- **Raw memory** — The user reads Mirror, not database entries.
