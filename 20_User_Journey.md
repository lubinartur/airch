# User Journey — A Day with AIRCH

> This is not a UX flow. It is a narrative.
> It tests whether the philosophy works in real life — not just on paper.

---

## The Person

Artur. 32. Building a startup. Lives in Tallinn. Has been using AIRCH for four months.

AIRCH knows him well by now — his spending patterns, his work rhythm, the projects he keeps returning to, the ones he keeps avoiding. It has noticed things he has not yet said out loud.

---

## 07:42 — Morning

Artur picks up his phone.

He opens AIRCH.

**Today** loads. One thought:

> The conversation with Mihkel is today. You have been avoiding preparing for it. That is probably the day.

No widgets. No task list. No health summary. Just that.

He knows AIRCH is right. He has been putting it off for three days.

He types in Dialogue: "I don't know what I want from the Mihkel conversation."

AIRCH:

> You have said that before — about the investor meeting in February, about the Marek situation last autumn. Each time the uncertainty was not about what you wanted. It was about whether you were willing to ask for it.
>
> What are you actually hoping he says yes to?

Artur sits with that for a minute.

He answers. The conversation continues for eight minutes. By the end he knows what he wants from the meeting.

AIRCH does not summarize the conversation. It does not say "great, you've got this." It closes when Artur closes it.

---

## 09:15 — At his desk

Artur opens the Work Space.

AIRCH's one-line read at the top:

> Three things are active. One is moving. Two have not been touched in eleven days.

Below: the three projects. Their last activity. What is open.

He looks at the one that is moving — AIRCH — and sees momentum. He looks at the two that are stalled. One he has been avoiding. One he genuinely forgot.

He does not need AIRCH to tell him which is which. He already knows.

He opens the stalled one. Reads the last note he left himself. Makes a decision: archive it. The project was never real — it was a way of feeling busy.

He types: "Archiving SkipMar. It was never going anywhere."

AIRCH in Dialogue, later that day:

> You have done this twice before — kept a project active long after you knew it was finished. Both times the decision to close it freed up something.

No lecture. One observation. Artur notes it.

---

## 12:30 — Finance Space

He checks Finance because a subscription renewal came through and he is not sure what it was.

He opens the Space.

AIRCH's read:

> Spending this cycle is running 18% above the last three. The difference is restaurants — concentrated in the last two weeks.

He knows why. The last two weeks were hard. He ate out to avoid thinking.

He does not need AIRCH to connect this to the work stress. He already sees it.

But later, in Dialogue, AIRCH says:

> The restaurant spending and the SkipMar stall overlap almost exactly. That has happened before — October, February. It is starting to look like a pattern.

Artur reads that twice.

*Чёрт. Он прав.*

---

## 16:00 — After the Mihkel meeting

The meeting went well. Better than expected.

Artur opens Dialogue.

"The meeting was good. He said yes."

AIRCH:

> What did you ask for?

Artur tells it. AIRCH listens — and stores it. Not as a transaction, but as a data point about what happens when Artur stops avoiding and asks directly.

It does not celebrate. It does not say "I told you so." It updates what it knows.

---

## 21:00 — End of day

Artur does not open AIRCH.

AIRCH does not send a notification. It does not check in. It does not ask how the day went.

It has enough. The meeting happened. The project was archived. The pattern was named.

Tomorrow, if something is worth saying, Today will say it.

Tonight, AIRCH is quiet.

---

## What This Journey Shows

**Today** worked because it had one thing to say — and said it directly.

**Dialogue** worked because it remembered February and October. Without that memory, it would have been a chatbot.

**Spaces** worked because they showed state without generating noise.

**Stillness** worked because AIRCH did not follow up at 21:00. That restraint is the product.

**Threads** worked without being named — the restaurant / SkipMar / stress connection surfaced naturally through Dialogue, not as a feature.

**Presence** worked because AIRCH sounded like the same entity across every interaction — calm, direct, memory-aware, never performing.

---

## What Did Not Appear

- No notifications
- No daily summary
- No health dashboard (nothing health-related was relevant today)
- No "here are your tasks"
- No "you have 3 unread insights"
- No encouragement
- No loading states that felt important
- No prompt to "explore" other sections

Everything that did not appear was a Stillness decision.

That is the product working correctly.
